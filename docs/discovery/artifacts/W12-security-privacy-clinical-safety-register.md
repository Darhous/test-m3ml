# Security, Privacy, and Clinical Safety Risk Register (Wave 12)

**Status: Draft.** Covers the 13 user-named risk categories (5 carried
forward from the Baseline/Constitution's own Open Risks, 8 newly surfaced
by the enterprise-wide expansion), each with full ID/Domain/Threat/Cause/
Impact/Severity/Owner/Mitigation/Detection/Escalation/Residual Risk/
Evidence/Status. This register is Discovery-scoped, feeding — not
replacing — `docs/constitution/REVIEW-REPORT.md`'s Open Risks and
`artifacts/RISK-REGISTER.md`.

## Consolidation Note

SEC-04 and SEC-08 are the same underlying risk (cross-tenant leakage)
approached from two angles (isolation-mechanism gap vs. data-access
threat) — consolidated to avoid inflating the risk count artificially, a
direct application of the discipline Wave 0 established when it fixed the
Baseline's risk-count discrepancy.

## Summary

**12 distinct risks** (13 named categories, 1 consolidated pair): 5
carried forward (all still open, none closed by this Wave — Discovery
cannot itself implement a rate-limiting policy or an incident-response
process), 7 newly surfaced. **2 risks rated Highest severity** (SEC-04/08
cross-tenant leakage, SEC-13 audit tampering) plus SEC-07 (unauthorized
verification) — all three tie directly to foundational trust guarantees
(tenant isolation, audit immutability, clinical authorization) rather than
to any single new enterprise domain.
